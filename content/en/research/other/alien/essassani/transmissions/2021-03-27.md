### Conversation 1: Abundance, Inequality, and Redefining Wealth

**Question (Kolina):** Hi my name is Kolina and I am from Colombia. I've seen most of the people struggle with things like payments, dues, health, and so on, meanwhile the 1% has all the money in the world. I don't think this is fair. You've taught us that everything changes in this world except for the laws. So I want to know, how can we as a collective change this in this lifetime? 

**(Bashar):** By broadening your definition of abundance and not focusing so much on the idea that money is the only form of it.

The idea is that if you equalize all forms of abundance—such as being given a gift, having something to trade, imagination, communication, synchronicity—then you can allow whatever form of abundance, including the idea of money as one representation of it, to come together in whatever amounts and percentages they need to form the 100% abundance that someone needs to continue to act on their excitement.

But because there's been such a high degree of focus on money being the only really valid form of abundance on your planet, you have this apparent unfair inequality. It only seems that way because you're only focusing on one aspect of abundance.

Abundance is simply the ability to do what you need to do when you need to do it, and there are many ways to allow that to happen.

When you start to relax and expand your definitions of abundance, you will find that you can be equally abundant as anyone you're calling the 1%, as long as you have the same capacity to do what you truly need to do when you need to do it. 

It doesn't matter what style of abundance you're using; you're still abundant equally. It will balance out. People who have more money will realize they don't need as much to do what they need to do, and then it will spread out a bit more evenly among the population. Everyone will be happy to use whatever form of abundance is necessary at any given moment, and the different forms will equalize themselves throughout the society.

### Conversation 3: Finding Your Passion Through Fear of Moving Forward

**Question (Alex from Thailand):**
Hello Bashar, my name is Alex, I'm from Thailand. I have a question about how to find my passion. Recently, I've been doing a lot of things that don't give me any motivation to keep on going, and I've been procrastinating for quite a long time because I couldn't find the things that give me the motivation. How can I discover my passion to do things that makes me money and gives me the motivation to keep on going? Thank you very much.

**Answer (Bashar):**
This might require a back-and-forth to really dig deeply into this subject, but at the same time we can say you are clearly passionate about procrastinating. So the idea is to find out what you're afraid will happen if you don't procrastinate. That would be simply based on a belief you have that something in your so-called future actions will actually cause you more pain, more difficulty, more suffering than simply holding back.

The idea is to get deeper into an investigation of your belief systems, because all of you are motivated absolutely to do those things you believe serve you and to never do the things that don't serve you. If you find that you're unable to move forward in the things you know could serve you, that must mean you have a belief system that says that moving forward would not serve you better than simply staying where you are.

Once you find out why you're holding onto that belief (which usually means you're afraid that something worse will happen if you do move forward), and you let that belief go as nonsensical because it's not a fact that that will happen, then you might start finding yourself more willing to act on things that are representative of your passion. You'll have reversed the motivational mechanism by no longer being afraid that moving forward will be worse than where you are.
