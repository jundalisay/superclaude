## Abundance and Letting Go

Abundance is simply the ability to do what you need to do when you need to do it. It doesn't need to include the concept of money. Although money exists on your planet as one of the valid ways you have of representing the concept of abundance, it isn't the only way. That sometimes is the block that many of you have had imposed upon yourselves by buying into certain belief systems about what it is to be abundant and what it is to not be abundant. Remember that it is simply the ability to do what you need to do when you need to do it. There are many ways to attract that condition in your life without having to insist that it come in one particular form.

Now, you have heard this talk many times in various disciplines about letting go, about surrendering in order to attain what it is you desire. Sometimes we find that in your society this can be a confusing issue, primarily because of all the different kinds of definitions that your society has bought into as to what letting go and surrendering and taking control actually means. If we can perhaps be of assistance in clarifying some of these issues, let us proceed in the following way.

You must remember that in your society for a long time you have been brought up to believe that when you want to achieve something, that this something is usually in your mind greater than what you are now. You create a separation, you create a differentiation between yourself as you are now and the thing you say you want to achieve, thereby putting yourself at a disadvantage by being at the bottom of a long, tall, high ladder that somehow you feel you must climb. No one is saying you cannot have challenge in your life. No one is saying that you will not want to attract mystery in your life to figure things out, to solve puzzles and problems, and to enjoy your ability to do these things. No one is saying you have to take that away. But simply what is being said is that when you think you have to reach for these things as if they truly were not a part of you, then you feel very often because of the definitions you have bought into that you somehow have to control your environment, control all the elements of your environment, all the circumstances of your environment, and sometimes even the people in your environment in order to achieve what you want. Because you do not understand and have not been taught to believe that the thing you want is already within you.

When you create that separation, then you think you have to go through something in order to get something that is somehow being kept from you. And so because you have also been taught that you are disconnected from existence, that you are not a part of existence, that existence is not an extension of you but somehow you are separate from it and contained within it, then you feel you have to control things. You feel you are at the mercy of that which you are not the creator of your reality, that you are its victim. Therefore the only way to attain what your heart desires is to somehow manipulate these random elements into some form of path that will represent the best ability you have of attaining what you want. But all of this very often for many of you seems like such a random occurrence, challenging the odds so to speak. And when you believe that way, then you get into this issue of control—that you have to control these things, that somehow all the necessary steps to get you from where you are to where you want to be can't happen automatically, can't simply effortlessly fall into place. Very often you don't believe it can happen that way because again in being taught that you are disconnected from reality, you are taught that at best you might achieve some measure of success. Don't you even have that as a phrase in your language—a measure of success? Not just success but a measure of success. "Well yes, I have achieved a measure of success in my lifetime." Constantly gauging yourself against some idea of how well you have been able to manipulate your environment in order to approximate some idea that you would like to attain but have been taught to believe is really unattainable in its pristine form, and that you are happy if you can get as close as you possibly can.

Now, letting go is really the first glimmering of understanding that life works already, that it is automatic—always things interacting with each other in perfect synchronization already. That every circumstance and every situation already comes as a complete kit with all the tools necessary contained therein automatically for the event that is desired to be manifest. The idea is to realize that if you can perceive it, you must already contain it. You cannot perceive what you do not contain. And if you understand this concept, then you will know that when you feel that you desire something above and beyond anything else, that the desire in and of itself contained all the information to allow you to manifest the desire. It is similar to the concept of a flower. The flower contains all the information to grow and to create a seed and to drop that seed and create another flower. The whole concept of the flower is already there. You have this idea on your planet: which came first, the chicken or the egg? The answer is neither came first. It is one concept, it is one idea. They are polarity expressions of one idea, and one cannot exist without the other. Neither came first or after; both were there simultaneously because one event was called for.

Look at it as we have used this analogy many times, as the idea of a coin. You don't say, "Well, which side of the coin came first, heads or tails?" The coin automatically has to have both sides or it's not a coin—it's nothing. Both sides are already there. You don't look at one coin on one side and turn it over and not expect to find the other side. "Well, I have one side of the coin, now how do I achieve the other side of the coin?" This is an analogy to what many of you do in your lives. You have this coin in your hand and you say, "Well, now what do I have to do to create the other side of the coin?" when of course all the time it's right there. But the process of turning the coin over to see that it's there is very often the issue of control and the struggle and effort you experience in your life. When if you really understood that once the concept is there, the whole concept is there—the desire and the desired thing is the same thing. Therefore instead of feeling that you have to exercise some control over a reality that will allow you to get to some place you are not at already, the idea is to begin to realize that what is meant by surrendering or letting go is surrendering and letting go of all of the assumptions and all the definitions that create a separation between one side of the coin and the other, to allow them to coexist simultaneously as one single concept. The moment it is formed, the observer and the observed are one and the same phenomenon.

Think about that. Understand that you are the observer. What you observe is you in some reflected format—it is you. So if you have a desire, if you have a goal you wish to attain, understand that all you're actually saying is you are attaining a clearer recognition of how it is you need to be in order to be that idea. So that by being that idea as fully as you can, then the manifestation of that idea will have no choice but to happen, because you will be being the reality and then it must manifest. Must manifest because you are the manifestation of the idea. And the way it usually occurs in your reality is that you simply start attracting all the circumstances, situations, opportunities, and people in your life flawlessly, effortlessly, smoothly that are representative of the desired goal, that allow you to act upon those opportunities and quote-unquote achieve the goal. It is an issue of being the goal itself rather than assuming that the goal is something you must attain that is not a part of you.

Now reduce the journey except for the parts you feel you need, except for the parts you feel you want to enjoy, except for the processes you feel are absolutely essential to the concepts and themes you chose to explore in this physical life. Except for those bare essentials that you know you simply want to have, you need no other extraneous side jaunts or shortcuts off to the side—shortcuts which very often take you much farther out of your way than simply moving straight ahead. Allow yourself the opportunity to recognize that letting go and surrendering does not mean giving up the issue of true control. It means really knowing you are already in control. The idea of the type of manipulative control that very often you are taught to believe means real control is an illusion in that sense. Because if everything already works—which it does—you don't need to exercise any extraneous control over it. In fact, to do so actually prevents it from working the way it was originally designed to do. Let your life work—it already does. You don't have to make it work; you just have to let it work. That's why you can relax, back off, let go, let go, let go.

I wish to point out as the final statement of this concept of letting abundance come into your life that the gesture you have in physical reality for letting go is exactly the same gesture it takes to hold as much as you can. When you try to control, you can hold very little. But when you let go, your arms are spread wide, and by analogy that means you are ready to receive as much as you can possibly hold. And that's when you are given abundance by the universe. The universe cannot give you abundance if you are like this because I have nothing to hold it with. So let go. Show the universe your arms are open wide and you are ready to receive all that it has to give you. Trust that analogy. You know what you're doing when you make that motion. Let go, receive. Letting go and receiving is the same gesture.

Remember that we will thank you at this time also for allowing us to experience the abundance of all the different perspectives and reflections of consciousness that you have created upon your planet, allowing us to experience you individually and collectively. We thank you for the gift of allowing us to share with you our perspective and for sharing with us yours in return. For the gift of this exchange, I ask you now, in what way may I be of service to you?
