**The Cutout Exercise**

Take an illustration of either a hybrid or a gray that is provided for you to download in this transmission. Cut it out, blow it up, and paste it on some stiffer background—cardboard, foam core, or whatever you wish that allows it to be rigid. Blow it up to the point where the head of the image of the gray or the hybrid (from the top of the crown to the bottom of the chin) fills one of your typical 8.5 by 11-inch pages. Allow the rest of the neck and the shoulders to be whatever size they will be by following that measurement.

Once it has been placed on a rigid background, place that cutout outside one of your windows (or more than one) as if it is peeking through the window at you. The goal is to get used to the idea, as you are simply going about your daily business, of now and then catching that image out of the corner of your eye, or more directly, especially at nighttime, peeking in at you. This allows you to get used to the idea over time that there are interactions going on between those beings and yourself.

As you become more used to seeing them, allow yourself no longer to be startled by those particular images. Start to relate to those images as if they are real three-dimensional beings looking at you physically through your window. This technique will allow you more ability to accept that these beings are in your reality and allow you more efficacy at actually being able to perceive the real beings as they enter your reality, observe you, and start to interact with you. This is for the purpose of making sure that you can begin to experience, from this point forward, those interactions on a more conscious basis, on your own terms, as equals, without fear. This allows you to not only remember some of the encounters you've already had that you may have forgotten (whether suppressed by the Grays/Hybrids or by your own fear), but also to become more aware and conscious of any future encounters.

### Beliefs, Fear, and Reality Creation

**Question:** Many people say your teachings and the teachings of Abraham are alike, however, I seem to find many differences. For example, they say that whatever you think and feel is what you attract. However, you say beliefs are what generate thoughts and feelings and therefore create reality. When I listened to them, I was obsessed with monitoring my thoughts 24/7, freaking out if a negative thought came up. You taught us to observe them neutrally to transform and integrate them. Also, I had a car crash while listening to them, supposedly vibrating high. What is your perspective?

**Bashar:** Different teachers have different ways of reaching different people. There are people that gravitate to certain semantics and words. Other teachers may supply other aspects to give a different understanding. Taken all together, you have a complete picture, but that doesn't mean the same delivery comes through every source. It is important for you, as you evolve, to pick the pieces out of any source that work for you and leave the rest behind. You may gravitate to one teacher at one time and another at another time because you are piecing together what works for you to create the path of least resistance for who you truly prefer to be. It is not necessarily a contradiction; you are simply looking at things from different points of view using methods that appeal to different belief systems at different times.

Regarding the car crash: On a simplistic physical level, it may have been a lesson to pay attention to what you are doing physically, because you can get hurt if you are distracted. On other levels, you were in a state of being where you realized that no matter how dangerous the situation seemed, you would emerge unscathed. Being in that state allowed you to experience a different reality than what you allowed the car to experience, proving that physical reality can, in some sense, be circumvented.

It is not always wise to assume that just because you believe you are on a certain level, certain physical things can't happen. The basic concept is: **It’s not about what happens; it’s about what you do with what happens.** That shows what level you are on and what vibrational state you are in. The idea is not that a car crash shouldn't have happened because you were on a high vibrational state; that is an insistence and assumption. Just about anything can happen no matter what level you are on. What matters is your response.

### **Q&A Session 2: Childhood Fear and the "Glass Wall"**

**Question:** I was terrified of alien contact as a child. Why? And how does the "glass wall" effect apply to seeing others making choices I don't prefer?

**Bashar:** As children, when many of these contacts actually start happening, you have never been taught that such a thing is going on in your reality. You haven't been given the tools to handle it. When you are shifted to different frequencies of reality representative of these beings, your physical mind doesn't understand what is going on. You feel out of control, even to the point of going into survival mode, because the physical personality feels like it is being annihilated or dying when shifted from its typical reality. We deliver the tools necessary to incorporate an understanding of how reality works, giving you the ability to calm yourselves and realize that, from a higher level, you made a choice to participate in this.

Regarding the "glass wall": Just because you see people making choices that represent other realities doesn't mean you are in the same reality. Everything you see is relevant, but perhaps in a different way. Seeing someone making choices you don't prefer adds greater clarity to the choices you *do* prefer. Even if something triggers an emotional response like fear, that is an opportunity to look at what beliefs are arising.

**Question:** Some beliefs keep reappearing, like "I'm wrong" or "I shouldn't exist." They feel sticky. How do I deal with them?

**Bashar:** You must investigate more deeply what you are getting out of holding on to those things. There must be a belief that says there is some advantage to remembering or recreating this idea. For example, you might believe that if you don't keep reminding yourself, you might forget and fall into a regimen you don't prefer. You might be using it as a guidance system to ensure you choose the direction you prefer. If you continue to believe you need that reminder, it will constantly be there. Investigate what you believe you are getting out of it.

One person realized their belief was: *"It's keeping me small so I'm not seen."*
**Bashar:** Why don't you want to be seen? What are you afraid will happen if you are seen?
**Response:** It feels dangerous, like someone is going to come get me.
**Bashar:** This is the result of a misunderstanding of how frequencies work. If you are actually in the frequency of your true self/passion, there cannot, by definition, be anything dangerous in that. If you feel something dangerous might happen if you act on your passion, you are already out of the vibration of your passion. You are conflating the definition of passion with a contradictory vibration. You can only experience the fear of something happening if you have already left the vibration of your true self.

Being in the state of passion doesn't mean you won't see things that symbolize danger; it means you will respond to them differently. As you expand your consciousness, you don't become less aware of the negative; you become *more* aware of it, but less likely to choose it as your experiential reality.

### **Q&A Session 4: Soul Traps, Archons, and Negative Entities**

**Question:** I was told there are negative beings influencing me through gangstalking, loosh harvesting, Archons, and a Demiurge. They trick us with fake tunnels of light to keep us in an Earth Matrix trap. How can we know this creation isn't a trap?

**Bashar:** That is basically a story. First, examine reports of near-death experiences or transmissions from spirits; you will hear absolutely none of this from them. Second, philosophically: What is the definition of a trap? A trap is something that only works when you *don't know* it's there. The fact that you are aware of the story of a "soul trap" demonstrates that there is no such trap, because if there really were, you wouldn't know about it—you would just be trapped.

The irony is that the trap, if there is one, is the *story itself*. Believing there is a trap is the trap. Now that you know how traps work, don't fall for it. Change your vibration, and you will no longer be equal to the idea that there is a trap.
