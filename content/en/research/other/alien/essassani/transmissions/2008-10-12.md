## Part One: The Download and Action Connection

**Bashar:** Last night of your time, we spoke about the idea of downloads. We will reiterate a few points briefly because there is connection between the idea of download and take action.

We spoke about the idea of the communion between the higher self as it downloads to the physical brain, which then transmits to the physical mind information. We reminded you that the higher mind conceives, the brain receives, the physical mind perceives. We talked about the mechanism by which that occurs, involving the pineal gland and the neurochemical transmitters it creates to facilitate that connection between higher non-physical mind through the physical brain into the personality construct.

The reason we remind you of the idea of the download—all the information that comes through the conduit of imagination, inspiration, and communion with the higher mind—is that the idea is a two-part process. Downloading is the first part, but taking action is the second part.

Once again, by analogy, you can liken it to the idea of electricity. If you are receiving information, if you are receiving inspiration, if you do not actually flow that information through you—apply it by physical action in your physical reality—then there is really no point in the continuation of the downloading of information. Therefore, the downloaded information can simply stay within you, build up within you, and the downloads in a sense can seem to cease, for there is no reason to pick up any more information if you haven't already used what you've been given.

So it is crucial to understand that in order to really use the information, to allow more information to come through, taking action is very important in your physical reality.

## Expectation vs. Expectancy

**Bashar:** Once again, when we talk about the idea of taking action, it is within the context of understanding that it is to be without specific expectation. You can have, for lack of a better term, the expectancy that it will bring you what you need, what you require—it will increase, accelerate, and expand your joy, your creativity, your passion. But the expectation refers to the specific way in which you might envision a thing coming to fruition.

Your physical mind does not really have the capability of knowing exactly how a thing may manifest. But it does have the capability of envisioning a symbolic manifestation in your mind's eye that represents the idea of that particular reality that you desire, that you prefer.

We are not saying that what your physical mind might imagine as a picture that represents that reality you prefer cannot manifest. It can, and sometimes often does, exactly as you have pictured it. But what we are reminding you of is that whatever it is the physical mind is capable of conjuring up as an image of that so-called preferred reality—that idealized reality, that desired reality—is not necessarily the highest depiction of that reality you actually desire, of that state of being. The physical mind can only imagine so much in the context of its physical reality.

Whatever it is you picture as a vision of the reality you prefer, you can use that as a template, as a guide. Use it to allow yourself to feel, to identify and feel the vibration, the excitement, the passion that represents that reality. But please remember that whatever picture you have conjured from your physical mind may seem to you the highest possible representation of that idea, but to the higher mind it's the lowest possible representation. The higher mind will take that as a foundation and can go up from there, actually giving you something manifest, something for you that is far greater, far more exciting, far more imaginative than you were capable of imagining with the physical mind.

That's why it is important to not insist with your expectation that the vision your physical mind conjures for you must be the way it occurs. If you insist on it, you're actually stopping the higher mind from giving it to you in a way that might be better than you imagined. Just use it as a guide, knowing that it is the vibration the higher mind needs to key off of to then bring to you what you are willing to allow to come to you.

## Practical Application: Choosing What to Act On

**Bashar:** When we talk about the idea of taking action on your excitement, please remember it doesn't mean you have to have some sort of particular life-spanning career or project in mind. You can, that's fine, but you don't have to start there if you don't have one in mind.

The idea, first and foremost, is to recognize that at any given moment you have available to you a number of options of things you could do. All you have to do is look at all the options available to you and act on the one that is most exciting to you—that you have the greatest degree of ability to take action on at that moment.

If more than one seems equally exciting, you can either take action on either and let it guide you as to whether to keep going in that direction, turn around and do the other one, or you can relabel, redefine, and re-identify the two apparently equally exciting things as simply different aspects of one larger overriding exciting thing.

The first criteria: choose whatever it is that is the most exciting. Secondly, that you have the greatest degree of ability to actually do something about, and take it as far as you can until you can take it no further. When you reach that new crossroads, look around for what opportunities, circumstances, and situations are once again most representative of the most exciting thing, choose that, and act on it.

It's not about looking for those things that you think are supposed to appear connected to what you think excites you. It's simply about following whatever is most exciting and knowing that the reason it's most exciting is because it is connected to where you want to go. That's why it's exciting—not because it has to look like it's connected. Very often the things that excite you the most will not necessarily appear to be connected to other things that excite you, but they may actually in fact be representative, moving in that direction of the path of least resistance.

Your physical mind may think the path of least resistance is this way, but your higher mind will show you, through the idea of what comes with the most excitement, what the path of least resistance actually is—even if it seems a little circuitous at first. The excitement tells you it's actually the shortest, quickest, most effortless path. Trust the excitement. Trust the passion. It will lead you in the proper direction, allow you to process what you need to process, allow you the synchronicity you need to experience, and get you where you're going in the best possible way with the highest amount of ability and appreciation to use what you manifest.

## Mocking Up the Vision

**Bashar:** We have also talked about the idea of creating as solidly as possible the vision that you have in your mind of what represents your ideal reality. We have talked about the idea of mocking it up in physical terms, simulating it if you will, as best as you can. This is still an important key.

Many people who consider themselves to be successful manifestors will have, at various points in their lives, had a vision—but they will actually have play-acted the vision out physically in some fashion, even though it may not actually have been, at the moment, the "real" thing.

They will go through the actual physical motion, mocking it up as though they really are at that moment making a real movie, doing it for real, being a real actor.

By doing that, by taking that kind of action, not only acting on the things you are capable of acting on that apparently relate to the thing you prefer, but also mocking it up and acting through that scenario, you train your body consciousness to get used to that vibration of that reality. The more real you can make it, the more real your brain and your body will believe that it is. The more real your brain and body believe it is, the easier it will be for you to align with your higher mind and allow the higher mind to manifest that reality for you.

We recognize that even though we have discussed this idea many times, many of you will still think we are talking metaphorically and will just do the exercise in your mind and will not actually go to the length to actually physically transform your physical environment to actually be as close a representation of the reality you prefer. We are urging you strongly to actually take action in the direction of creating in your reality some representation as close to the vision of that reality as you can—something that is physically present, to make it look as much like that reality as possible.

Key off of those representations as though they are real, which is why it's important to make them look as real as possible. Use your imagination—not just to sit around and use it, but use your imagination while you are physically mocking it up, to play-act through that and get used to it.
