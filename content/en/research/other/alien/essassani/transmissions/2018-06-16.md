Time is a side effect of your consciousness shifting over and over again to different points of view of the same single moment of existence, making it appear to be different moments. You can use the analogy we have used before: these different moments are themselves parallel realities that can be illustrated as frames on a film strip. Each frame is its own reality, but it has no movement in it, no experience, no awareness, and no self-reflectivity. Each frame is a single, static snapshot of a potential state of being.

Therefore, when you shift your perspective—your consciousness—through the different frames one after another, like a projector light through a film strip, you get the illusion of continuity. This creates the illusion of movement, time, space, change, growth, and difference.

If you look at the handouts, you see squares. In Group A, the arrow moves relatively straight from left to right. This is usually how your consciousness perceives the idea: as a smooth continuity, one moment after the next, in a logical, continuous progression. However, more likely and more commonly, the picture is actually like Group B. You are actually jumping all over the place into different frame moments as you determine for yourself what the next experiential frame ought to be that serves you best, based on your belief system at that moment.

Even though you may be jumping around, it will still feel like Group A. It will feel like you are just moving straight ahead, frame after frame, in a logical continuous progression. However, as you start to expand your consciousness and become more aware that you are creating the illusion of time and space, you may begin to experience what we call "breaking continuity." This can be experienced as things happening in an accelerated way, where you don’t necessarily experience the in-between moments. It’s a "how did I get here?" moment. These slippages of space and time are the result of your consciousness becoming more aware of the fact that you are jumping around all the time anyway.

This is the basic framework for what we are going to discuss regarding time travel.

### **Topic 1: The Mechanics of Time Travel and Parallel Realities**

**Bashar:**
The idea that time travel works the way science fiction stories portray it is not really how it works. It is all about shifting to parallel realities that may be very similar to your own, so you might not immediately tell the difference.

Look at Example A in your handout. A person goes from baby to child to teen to adult. As an adult, they invent time travel and decide to go back to see their younger self. You would think, as most sci-fi proposes, that you are actually going back to *your* childhood. You are not. If in your original childhood frames you never met an older version of yourself, those frames are fixed. You cannot change those frames.

When you have the experience of traveling back and running into your younger self, that is taking place in a *parallel reality* where that child *did* meet you. You have become part of that child’s timeline (Timeline 2). When you decide to go back to your own time, you can’t return to the exact frame you left because that frame doesn’t include you anymore—it’s blank. You will find yourself in a different parallel reality, perhaps as the senior of that reality, because you’ve changed your own experience of history by meeting your childlike self, which didn’t happen in the primary timeline. You are skipping all over the place, shifting your consciousness to become another version of yourself in another parallel reality.

Now, look at the concept of actual time travel within your own timeline. If you literally went back in time in your own timeline, you wouldn’t meet your child self; you *would be* your child self. Your consciousness exists all the way up and down the timeline already. Literally traveling back means you simply experience yourself as the child again, with no idea what you are going to grow up to be. It’s like living it over again in a loop. You would not know you had time traveled; you would simply be experiencing being the child exactly as you did before.

What you call "memory" is simply allowing your consciousness to slide back down the timeline and experience the idea of the child, but without committing fully to having time traveled physically. You remain the adult having the experience of remembering. Doing this in the opposite direction, toward the future, is what you call being psychic or predicting the future. You are sensitive enough to figure out the probabilities of what might happen among the many parallel realities.
