## The Nature of Art and Artistic Expression

You have expressed yourself in a variety of creative ways, and much that is created when shared with your public is considered what you call Art, in that it evokes a mood, ideas, and allows people to engage in an expression of the artist that is representative not only of the individual artist that has created the piece in whatever form it may come—painting, sculpture, drawing, writing, music, doesn't matter—but also, of course, is an expression of the time in which it is created, and also of the collective consciousness of the society in which it is created.

So art is quite subjective and is capable of evoking many different kinds of reactions, reflections, moods, ideas within different individuals, because first and foremost, art is a mirror.

Whether or not they may know it, those that look upon, or listen to, or receive in whatever form the artistic expression, are bringing to it their own subjective point of view, their beliefs, their ideas, their feelings, their moods, attitudes, energies, and are getting a reflection from that mirror back to themselves.

An opportunity to investigate why they react or respond the way they do. What does the art mean to the one looking at it? How does it put you in touch with either other aspects of your own Consciousness or other aspects of the Consciousness and expressions of the society in which you live? What does it Inspire within you to express from yourself, to share with others, to converse, to discuss?

Art in that sense allows you to investigate aspects of your being that you may actually be unconscious of, and bring them to the Forefront by looking at your response, your reaction to what you are experiencing. And also allow you to create a response, a creative response in some way shape or form that would be the inspiration part. But first and foremost, the idea is that art—even though it may seem to be very passive in that you're just looking or listening at whatever the piece of art is—is actually quite active, because it activates certain things within each individual that participates, that is willing to open up and discover more of themselves by looking in that mirror as it has been presented to them by the artist.

So that you gain, as we said, not only insight into the artist, into the society, into the timing, but also into yourself. And ideally allow that when it bounces around inside you, the ideas, the feelings, the moods that it engenders and inspires within you, to let it out in an expression of equal Artistry, equal creativity in some way shape or form, so that it doesn't just remain a passive event, so that you are inspired to express the art within yourself in whatever way it may be relevant for you.


## Permission Slips for Artistic Expression: The Mirror Exercise

Stand in front of a mirror and just gaze at yourself for a moment. First and foremost, appreciate the Artistry of your being. 

Whether it be just the physical form, whether it be the look in your eyes, the expression on your face, just appreciate what you're seeing, what you are being given as a reflection.

Because it will be an opportunity for you to begin to understand the uniqueness that each of you is, and thus the artist that each of you is. 

And we say that because for a long time in your Society, most of you have been taught to believe that you had nothing at all to do with the way you are, with the way you look, with the way you appear. But you are the artist of yourself. We have said before, and we will say now again, that because you are really all in your Natural State in spirit, and physical reality is in a sense just a projection or a dream, you are in fact channeling these personalities into physical reality. 

So you are channeling yourself all the time, you are inventing yourself all the time, projecting this artificial construct—this piece of art you call your body, your personality, your idea, your identity of yourself—all the time, and you're bringing that into being, and that is an artistic act.

### The Cube Exercise

Now here's a couple of other suggestions for you, a couple of other permission slips. Allow your artistic imagination to alter them however you wish.

Take a cube—can be a cube of wood, cube of any material, but a six-sided Cube.

- **On one face**, put a mirror, for the reasons that we just explained in the permission slip we just described.
- **On another face**, put a photograph of yourself at a younger age—if you have one; if you don't, draw something that's representative.
- **On another face**, a photo of yourself, say halfway through your life up to this point.
- **On another face**, a photo of yourself as you exist or believe yourself to exist right now.
- **On another face** of the cube, draw an empty shape that is representative of you and put a question mark in it.
- **On the sixth face**, allow yourself to Simply paint that entire face in your favorite color. That's all you have to do.

Now allow yourself to start with the image of just the outline and the question mark, and just look at it as representative of a blank you. You don't really know what you are. There's not really a reflection here. There's a mystery here about who you really are, what you really are. There's a curiosity to know deeply who you really are, what you really are as a being, as a Persona.

Then go to the picture of you, or the drawing of you, in your Youth and see where you believe you started. Gaze at it for a few minutes if you wish, few seconds—doesn't matter—but get a good sense of that child looking back at you.

Then go to the one that is in the middle—of the child and where you are now. Do the same thing—just engage it, look at it, feel it. If there are memories that come with it, that's fine, but just absorb what you're being given as you look at this reflection.

Then go to the one where you are now, look at it, engage it, stay with it a moment, learn from it if that's what happens.

Then go to the mirror and look at yourself as you believe yourself to be in that reflection at that moment.

Then flip it over and look at your favorite color.

Now go back to the mirror—see if anything has changed.

And go backwards through your present photo to the middle photo to the young photo to the face with the question mark. And then go forwards, do the same thing again, and backwards and forwards three times.

Do this and see if when you wind up looking at your reflection in the mirror at the end, if you don't see something new in your eyes, something new in your gaze, something that wasn't there before that is now peeking out at you—a hidden part of you, a new idea of you, a new angle, A New Perspective, a hint of someone there that is more than you consider yourself to be, a promise of something bigger, something grander, something creative and expressive, loving and passionate about life, about being the artistic expression that you are.

Do this and play with it. See what happens. Sometimes you may find that something subtle will happen. Sometimes you may actually be startled and see that something quite different is looking back at you from the mirror. Play with it. See what happens. Have fun with it.

All right, all right. Be the art of art itself, for you are created to be the Creator. So create consciously, create. Give over to that flow from yourself. It is most exhilarating, most rewarding, most revealing, and it will take you to a new level of your being.

We thank you for allowing us to share this with you this day. In return for the gift that you are giving us in allowing us to experience all of you, I ask now in return—in what way may we be of service to you? You may begin with your questions and dialogue if you wish.
