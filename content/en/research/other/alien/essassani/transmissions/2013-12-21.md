But the thing you really also need to understand is that when you make this kind of a list and you have the idea of the things that excite you and the things that don't—what you call the pros and the cons, the things you prefer and the things that you don't—many times in making this list, you will draw a line down the center and you say, "Well, the things that I prefer exist on this side of the line and the things that I do not prefer exist on this side of the line."

So one of the things you really need to understand, however, is that this line down the center is not a solid line. It is actually a permeable membrane.

And thus then, when you stay in the state of your excitement and your joy at the heading of the top of the list of things you prefer, then the things on the side—the negative side that you don't have, that you would love to manifest, that you prefer to manifest that are over there—the things you have listed there that you do not yet have as a manifestation because you haven't yet fully acted on your excitement—such as what you might tell: "Oh, I feel like I'm not being supported. But if you stay in the state of your excitement, then the things on that side of the line—the permeable line—will actually start to shift over to the other side.

Conversely, because it is a permeable membrane, if you stay in the energy state of negativity, of fear, then the things on the positive side will start to shift over to the negative side. This line in the middle can be crossed in both directions.

So you want to make sure, if this is what you prefer, that you stay in the vibration of your excitement so that the things that haven't yet manifested that you would prefer to manifest, that are representative of your dreams, will actually be able to cross through that permeable membrane, that line, and come onto the side of manifestation in a positive way.

So take inventory. Take stock of the things that you would prefer, the things that you believe you would prefer.

But remember, you also have to drop the expectation. This is one of the tools. But do the inventory. Remember all the tools, write them down that we have talked about. Clarify them for yourselves, because many times you may think you know the tool. You may even be holding the tool in your hand, but you may be holding it in an inappropriate manner. You may be holding the saw and wanting to cut that piece of wood, but if you're holding the saw upside down, you'll be dragging it across that piece of wood forever and ever and ever and ever, wondering why it never gets cut.

And then suddenly realize: "Wait a minute, that's not the way the tool is used. It needs to be used from a positive vibration, and then the teeth are facing down and I can cut through the board just like butter."

So allow yourself to remain in the state and take an inventory of all the things that you prefer to be. Do that inventory. It will be much easier for you to take stock of yourself, to really understand when you go off track, and to bring yourself back into that path.
