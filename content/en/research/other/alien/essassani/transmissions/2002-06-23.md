## The Paradox of External Change

### Do Not Fall into Judgment and Expectation

However, do not fall into the negative belief trap called judgment and expectation. What this means is as follows: Many people will go, "Well, I've changed my belief but I don't see anything outside in my life really changing. It doesn't look any different."

Once again, understand the nature of physical reality. All circumstances and situations in physical reality are fundamentally neutral. They do not have built-in meaning. A particular situation can create a positive or a negative effect. Any situation can actually create either a positive or a negative effect. The effect that you get out of any given situation is completely determined by the energy you put into it.

If you put positive energy into it, if you understand that the situation is there for a positive reason, you will only get a positive effect from it, no matter how it looks.

So let us say you have come out of a situation that you were feeling a negative effect from, and then you change your belief about that circumstance and situation. Then you go back into the situation, but on the surface nothing looks like it's changed. Do not despair and say, "Oh, nothing out here changed. That must mean I didn't really change my belief."

It's how you behave toward the situation that determines that your belief has changed—not what the situation looks like on the surface. When you trust, when you know you have changed your belief and you act differently within the situation, then even though the situation may look the same, you will get a different effect from the situation.

### The Testing Paradox

It is quite possible that the exterior situation may change its appearance to reflect the change within you, but it may not. You have to remain true within yourself to know you have changed and act like you know you have changed, and then you will get a different experience out of the very same situation.

And here is the paradox, here is the irony in this idea: If you look at the situation outside and say, "That means I haven't changed because it didn't change," then you haven't changed. In other words, if you're still relying on outward appearances to decide whether or not you have changed, then you really haven't changed, and it is reflecting an accurate picture to you.

But when you know you have changed and you accept that even though the thing may look the same you still believe you have changed, then you give the situation the greatest possibility of actually changing its outward appearance. But first, it's almost like you are testing yourself to see if you really believe you have changed by being given exactly the same situation again to see if you're going to act differently toward it.

So when you do behave differently toward it and get a different effect from it, then you have proved to yourself that you have changed, and then the outward situation may change its appearance to suit the true change that you now know exists within you.

But as long as you wait for your outer reality to change in order to believe that you have really changed, then you haven't really changed.
