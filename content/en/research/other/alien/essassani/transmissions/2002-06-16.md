### The Nature of Time and Reality

We will be speaking today about your world as it exists now and the many probable future ideas that may occur in time to come. But first, it is important when speaking of the future to always remember that there is no such thing as one set future. There are only many probable future realities that all coexist right now. The idea of past, present, and future is only an illusion, for there is no time. Time and space are just, if you will, side effects. They are side effects that occur in your mind as the result of the average of all the changes in creation. The experience of time and space is a side effect, then, of the result of the average of all the different possible past, present, and future ideas that all exist now.

The future is just a different "now" seen from a different perspective—or more precisely, it is the same "now" because there is only one moment, but it is seen from a different point of view. So, any future that we may discuss today already exists side by side with your experience of the present. Therefore, it is up to each and every one of you, individually and all of you together collectively, to decide which alternate "now" that you call the future you would prefer to experience. Thus, you can understand from this explanation that it is not a matter of waiting for the future to come to you; it is already here. It is simply up to you to decide which different parallel and simultaneous "now" moment you will experience next.
