## The Foundational Limitation of Mortality and Remembering Eternity

**Bashar:** We would like to begin this transmission with a brief idea we have discussed with many of you. We refer to the severe degree of limitation that your third-density reality has imposed upon its inhabitants for countless thousands of years. 

However, we would like to address an even more fundamental limitation. This is the underlying, foundational limitation instilled in you, sometimes even before birth, but usually from birth onward. It is one of the main reasons why, by the age of three, most of you are already telepathically locked in and synchronized to the limitations of your society. 

Between the ages of three and seven, you begin to forget who you really know yourself to be. You begin to take on the attributes of the forgetfulness of your collective society. Within that same timespan, if you have been exposed to them (as most of you have), you build up ideas of suppression, oppression, repression, and anger. You then spend much of your adult life attempting to locate and transform these ideas.

Many specific kinds of ideas add up to the suppressed and unconscious anger you eventually discover you are harboring. But it all really begins with the fundamental telepathic seeding of one particular idea within your consciousness when you are a newborn baby. 

When you come into physical reality, you still know—even if you cannot verbally communicate it in your culture's language—that you are truly, literally an eternal, infinite being. You know this because, as a spirit, you are eternal; you do not ever really cease to exist. 

Yet, from the moment you are physiologically born, you are inundated with the idea that you will someday die. You are told you are mortal, that you must endure limitation, suppression, suffering, and pain, ultimately winding up in death. But you know, coming in, that you are a free, unlimited, eternal being. You are presented with what might be euphemistically called the "first big lie": that you don't go on forever.

Now, we understand that you change, and that physiological death exists. But that is only a transformation. The essence of your consciousness is forever eternal and infinite. You begin to struggle with this dichotomy, not knowing at first how to translate the concept of mortal transformation into the concept of infinity. 

This sets up the first repressed anger in many of you: the idea that it is not fair. You know what is true—you know you will go on forever—but you are told you will not. You are trained to be who you are not, because the idea has grown up in your dimension of limitation that you must fit in a certain way to receive nurturing and survival tools. 

If you do not conform, the fear is that you will be abandoned by those you must rely on as a child. As you are spoon-fed these ideas of mortality and limitation, each bite sticks in your stomach. It is undigestible because you know it is not ultimately representative of the truth: that you are eternal, infinite, free, unlimited, and can be whoever you deem yourself to be.

You need to understand the parameters of physical reality and physiological death transitions. But what you also need to know, and be allowed to remember, is that you are eternal and will always exist, even if you change forms. Begin to teach your children this. Do not allow them to pick up on the telepathic idea of despair or the ending sense of death, except where constructive. 

Allow yourselves more freedom to be the children you were, and allow those children to grow into the adults they naturally would have become. Remember that you are facets of the infinite, eternal, and free.

We thank you for allowing us to remind you of this. We are not telling you anything new. You have known this forever. Now is the time of remembering, awakening, and gathering yourself back together into one holistic understanding. It is no longer a crime to be that way, and you no longer have to fear the retributions of society. 

This is the age of transformation. Take it upon yourself to decide who you are and live the life you dream you can live, instead of simply dreaming about being alive. We thank you for sharing this gift. In return, I ask: in what way may I and my civilization be of service to you through this transmission?
