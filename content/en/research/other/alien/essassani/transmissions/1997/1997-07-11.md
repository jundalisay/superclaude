In Essassani terms, that being our home world. Each Essani year, in our ancient history, we chose what would be called in your language a day of reflection. This can really be any day you wish it to be. It can be the day of your own birth, the anniversary of your birth. It can be any day you so desire.

But if you wish to follow our particular experience with this idea, it went something like this. On the day of reflection, whatever day that was that we chose it to be, we would take that day to, in a sense, detach from what might have been called the typical daily events. Take a day completely for ourselves to assess and reflect upon who we were, what we were doing, to determine whether or not we were who we wanted to be, to determine whether or not we were doing what we wanted to be doing. So that we would make sure we did not, in your terms, get caught up in assumptions and definitions and beliefs and patterns and actions that had nothing to do with our true natural core self.

So as a suggestion, you may wish to give this an attempt. To pick a day, make it your day of reflection, whatever day that may be, and to really slow down, take time, detach, look at yourself—if you wish, in the mirror—but look at yourself and really ask: Am I who I want to be? Am I doing the things that I really want to do, really prefer to do?

And if you find in any way, shape, or form that you may have gotten yourself caught up in other people's ideas of who you ought to be, what you should be doing, take a moment and really assess, recalibrate for yourself whether those things really are so important for you. And to discover for yourself what things really would be more representative of your own truth, your own core, your natural being, your own pace, your own timing. That which would truly be honest for you. To make an honest self-assessment, to relax into your true self, to not be impatient, not be in a hurry to get somewhere more important than where you are, to be someone more important than who you naturally are.

Because when you finally allow yourself to truly relax into your true natural self, as you were originally created to be, in a sense, as you were originally designed to be, then you will always find—when you do that—that natural you will be automatically and naturally creative, joyous, happy, loving, spontaneous, inspirational, and inspired. All the things that are the natural reflection of being in synchronous harmony with Creation, with the unconditional love of the Infinite, as you may term it in your language.

When you relax into yourself, your true self, your core self, then you will be able to, in a sense, clarify the things you really need to do and recognize the things you don't really need to do. To be yourself and to allow your life to unfold in a magical, natural, automatic, effortless, creative way.

Allow yourself this day of reflection. All day, all day, of course. If you wish, take two days, take three. But at least allow a day—a day of reflection that honors just you, for you, created by you. Be with yourself and find out what your real true self is.

Let go of all the presuppositions about what you think you're supposed to be. Examine your actions, examine your patterns, and find out which really belong to you—the real you—and which perhaps might simply belong as leftovers, holdovers from parents, peers, and other factions of society that may have nothing to do with you.

We are not saying that you have to throw all advice or all suggestion from others into the trash. No. But simply take a moment to decide which really are true for you and in what way to allow suggestions, reflections, and advice from others to serve you. Make your own determinations based on who you really feel that you are.

And when you do relax into your natural self, you will know how to recognize who the real you is. You will know what feels like you and what does not. You will begin to sense more strongly and get used to the idea what vibration, what frequency is synonymous with your true being and which is not. Allow yourself to fall into harmony with who you are.

Take this time, this day of reflection, so that then in any future reflection, anytime you look at yourself metaphorically or literally in any kind of mirror, the you you see looking back will be the real you at that moment. And you will see that it will always be a face relaxed and joyful and happy to be alive in gratitude, in self-expression, and in unconditional love.

This is a ritual, if you wish, that has worked well for our society. We no longer really use it because now every day for us is a day of self-reflection. And every day we choose only those things that are our natural selves, and thus it has become our way of life, automatically, instinctively.

And so you can begin in that. Your society is where we were some time ago, generations ago. This can serve you to recalibrate you, to give you that moment of pause, that moment of balance, to remember—to remember who you are, who the real you is—while you are playing the game of physical reality and taking on the clothing and personification of different masks, different faces, different agendas, different ideas. It always helps to remember who is really at the core of that experience, because that's what it's all about.

The experience of being yourself. That's what your lives are all about. That's the goal, the purpose, the mission, the function—is the experience of being who you are, your unique facet of the Infinite. And that's all there is to it.

So take time to have a little more fun and to learn that being yourself is really the most fun thing you can be, and certainly the thing that actually ultimately requires the least effort of all.

We thank you for allowing us to reflect this idea to you this day of your time. And in return for the gift that you are giving our civilization in co-creating this communication, we ask: in what way may we now be of service to you?
