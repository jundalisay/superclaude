<sup>1</sup> When Isaac was old, and his eyes were dim, so that he could not see, he called Esau, his eldest son, and said to him:

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>2</sup> I am old, I know not the day of my death.

<sup>3</sup> Take your weapons, your quiver and your bow, and go out to the field, and take me [some] venison.

<sup>4</sup> Make me savoury meat, such as I love, and bring [it] to me, that I may eat; that my soul may bless you before I die. 
{{< /l >}}


<sup>5</sup> Rebekah heard this, Esau went to the field to hunt for venison.

<sup>6</sup> Rebekah told Jacob her son:

{{< r a="Rebekah" >}}
I heard your father tell Esau <sup>7</sup> to bring him venison and make him a savoury meat so that he can bless him before Anu before his death. 

<sup>8-9</sup> Go now to the flock, and fetch me from there 2 good kids of the goats. I will make them savoury meat for your father, the kind he loves.

<sup>10</sup> You shall bring it to your father for him to eat and bless you before his death. 
{{< /r >}}



{{< r a="Jacob" >}}
<sup>11</sup> Esau is a hairy man, and I am a smooth man. <sup>12</sup> My father will feel me. I shall seem to him as a deceiver and bring a curse on me, not a blessing. 
{{< /r >}}

{{< r a="Rebekah" >}}
<sup>13</sup> Your curse will be on me. Obey me, and go fetch me the goats.
{{< /r >}}

<sup>14</sup> He went and brought them to his mother who then made savoury meat that his father loved.

<sup>15</sup> She:
- took goodly clothing of her eldest son Esau, which were with her in the house, and put them on Jacob her younger son.
- <sup>16</sup> put the skins of the kids of the goats on his hands, and on the smooth of his neck.
- <sup>17</sup> gave the savoury meat and the bread, which she had prepared, into the hand of her son Jacob.

<sup>18</sup> He came to his father, and said:

{{< r a="Jacob" >}}
My father, here I am.
{{< /r >}} 

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
Who are you, my son? 
{{< /l >}}

{{< r a="Jacob" >}}
<sup>19</sup>  I am Esau your firstborn. I have done according as you bid me. Please sit and eat of my venison, that your soul may bless me.
{{< /r >}}

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>20</sup> How could you have found it so quickly, my son? 
{{< /l >}}

{{< r a="Jacob" >}}
Because Anu brought it to me. 
{{< /r >}}

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>21</sup> Come near so that I may feel you whether you are Esau or not.
{{< /l >}}

<sup>22</sup> Jacob went near to Isaac who felt him and said:

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
The voice is Jacob’s voice, but the hands are the hands of Esau.
{{< /l >}}

<sup>23</sup> He discerned him not, because his hands were hairy, as his brother Esau’s hands: so he blessed him. 

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>24</sup> Are you my son Esau? 
{{< /l >}}

{{< r a="Jacob" >}}
Yes, I am.
{{< /r >}}

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>25</sup> Bring it near to me, and I will eat of my son’s venison, that my soul may bless you.
{{< /l >}}

He brought it near to him, and he did eat: and he brought him wine, and he drank. 

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>26</sup> Come near now, and kiss me, my son. 
{{< /l >}}

<sup>27</sup> He came near, and kissed him. He smelled his clothes and blessed him and said:

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
See, the smell of my son is as the smell of a field which Anu had blessed. <sup>28</sup> Therefore Anu give you:
- the dew of heaven
- the fatness of the earth
- plenty of corn and wine

<sup>29</sup> Let people serve you, and nations bow down to you: be lord over your brethren. Let your mother’s sons bow down to you: cursed be every one that curses you. Blessed be he that blesses you.
{{< /l >}}

<sup>30</sup> As soon as Isaac had finished blessing Jacob, Esau came in from his hunting.

<sup>31</sup> He also had made savoury meat, and brought it unto his father, and said to his father:

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
Father please arise, and eat of my venison, that your soul may bless me. 
{{< /r >}}


<sup>32</sup> Isaac asked him: Who are you?

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
I am your son, your firstborn Esau. 
{{< /r >}}


<sup>33</sup> Isaac trembled very exceedingly, and said:

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
Who? Where [is] he who had taken venison and brought it me? I have eaten of all it before you came, and have blessed him.
{{< /l >}}

<!-- yea, [and] he shall be blessed.  -->


<sup>34</sup> When Esau heard the words of his father, he cried with a great and exceeding bitter cry, and said to his father:

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
Bless me also, O my father.
{{< /r >}}


<sup>35</sup> Your brother came with subtilty, and had taken away your blessing. 

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
<sup>36</sup> Was it Jacob? He had supplanted me these 2 times. 
1. He took away my birthright
2. Now, he has taken away my blessing.

Have you not reserved a blessing for me? 
{{< /r >}}

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>37</sup> I have made him your lord. All his brethren have I given to him for servants. With corn and wine have I sustained him. What shall I do now to you, my son?
{{< /l >}}

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
<sup>38</sup> Have you but one blessing, my father? Bless me too, O my father.
{{< /r >}}


Esau lifted up his voice, and wept. 

Isaac replied:

{{< l a="Isaac" >}}
<sup>39</sup> Your dwelling shall be the fatness of the earth, and of the dew of heaven from above.

<sup>40</sup> By your sword shall you live and serve your brother. It shall come to pass when you shalt have the dominion, that you shalt break his yoke from off your neck.
{{< /l >}}

<sup>41</sup> Esau hated Jacob because of the blessing wherewith his father blessed him. Esau said in his heart:

{{< r a="Esau" >}}
The days of mourning for my father are near. Then will I slay my brother Jacob. 
{{< /r >}}


<sup>42</sup> Rebekah was told what Esau had said.

{{< r a="Rebekah" >}}
[To Jacob] Behold, your brother Esau, wants to kill you. <sup>43</sup> Flee to Haran to my brother Laban. <sup>44</sup> Stay with him a few days, until your brother’s fury goes away.

<!-- Until your brother’s anger turn away from you, and he forget [that] which you hast done to him: -->
<sup>45</sup> Then I will send, and fetch you from there. Why should I be deprived also of you both in one day?
{{< /r >}}

{{< r a="Rebekah" >}}
[To Isaac] <sup>46</sup> I am weary of my life because of the Hittite women [daughters of Heth]. If Jacob takes a Hittite wife, such as these which are of the daughters of the land, my life will not be worth living.
