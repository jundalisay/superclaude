{{< l a="Anu" >}}
<sup>1</sup> [To Jacob:] Go to Bethel. Live there and make there an altar for Anu. I appeared to you when you fled from the face of Esau your brother. 
{{< /l >}}


<sup>2</sup> Then Jacob said to his household:

{{< l a="Jacob" >}}
Put away the strange idols that are among you. Be clean and change your garments.

<sup>3</sup> Let us go up to Bethel. I will make there an altar unto Anu, who answered me in the day of my distress, and was with me in the way which I went. 

<sup>4</sup> They gave unto Jacob all the strange idols which they had, and all their earrings in their ears. Jacob hid them under the oak which was by Shechem.

<sup>5</sup> They journeyed. The terror of Anu was upon the cities that were around about them. They did not pursue the sons of Jacob.

<sup>6</sup> So Jacob and his people came to Luz in Canaan, that is Bethel.  

<sup>7</sup> He built there an altar, and called the place El-beth-el: because there Anu appeared unto him, when he fled from the face of his brother. 

<sup>8</sup> But Deborah, Rebekah’s nurse, died. She was buried beneath Bethel under an oak which was called Allon-bachuth.

<sup>9</sup> Anu appeared unto Jacob again, when he came out of Padan-aram, and blessed him.


{{< l a="Anu" >}}
<sup>10</sup> Your name is no longer Jacob, but Israel. 

<sup>11</sup> I am the Lord Almighty. Be fruitful and multiply. You shall be a nation and a company of nations. Kings shall come out of your loins.

<sup>12</sup> I will give you and to your seed the land which I gave Abraham and Isaac.
{{< /l >}}


<sup>13</sup> Anu went up from him in the place where he talked with him. 


<sup>14</sup> Jacob set up a pillar in the place where he talked with Anu, even a pillar of stone. He poured a drink offering thereon, and he poured oil thereon. 

<sup>15</sup> Jacob called the name of the place where Anu spake with him, Bethel.

<sup>16</sup> They went from Bethel; there was but a little way to come to Ephrath: and Rachel travailed, and she had hard labour.

<sup>17</sup> When she was in hard labour, that the midwife said unto her, Fear not; you shalt have this son also. 18} And it came to pass, as her soul was in departing, (for she died) that she called his name Ben-oni: but his father called him Benjamin. 

<sup>19</sup> Rachel died, and was buried in the way to Ephrath, which is Bethlehem. 

<sup>20</sup> Jacob set a pillar upon her grave: that [is] the pillar of Rachel’s grave unto this day.

<sup>21</sup> Israel journeyed, and spread his tent beyond the tower of Edar.

<sup>22</sup> When Israel dwelt in that land, Reuben went and had sex with Bilhah, his father’s concubine. Israel heard it.

Jacob has 12 sons:

<sup>23</sup> The sons of Leah:

1. Reuben, Jacob’s firstborn
2. Simeon
3. Levi
4. Judah
5. Issachar
6. Zebulun

<sup>24</sup> The sons of Rachel:

7. Joseph
8. Benjamin

<sup>25</sup> The sons of Bilhah, Rachel’s handmaid:

9. Dan
10. Naphtali

<sup>26</sup> The sons of Zilpah, Leah’s handmaid:

11. Gad
12. Asher

These are the sons of Jacob born to him in Padan-aram.

<sup>27</sup> Jacob came to Isaac his father unto Mamre, unto the city of Arbah, which is Hebron, where Abraham and Isaac sojourned. 

<sup>28</sup>Isaac lived for 180 years and <sup>29</sup> died and was gathered unto his people, being old and full of days.

His sons Esau and Jacob buried him.
