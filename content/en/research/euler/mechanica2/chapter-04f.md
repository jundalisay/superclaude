## Scholium

875. But not only can the motion of a body on the surface of an erect cylinder be easily
determined with the help of resolution, but also, if the axis of the cylinder should be
horizontal, with the same ease the motion of the body becomes known. 

If the body does not have a horizontal motion along the cylinder, then the body perpetually remains on the same section of the cylinder as that is moving on a given line. But if now
the motion is agreed to be horizontal, this remains the same always and does not disturb the other motion; and from these motions taken together the motion of the body truly
becomes known easily.
