## Socratic Socialism

The socialism of Socrates is has similarities and differences. For example, Socrates pushed for communal living and communal property (instead of private property) for the ruling guardians. 

Socrates' policies are based on the four desires of the human species: 

1. Freedom
2. Control
3. Ideas
4. Resources 

This leads to the 4 virtues:

1. Temperance
2. Courage
3. Truth 
4. Justice


{{< q a="Athenian" c="The Laws by Plato, Book 1" >}}
Justice, temperance, and wisdom, when united with courage, are better than courage only.
{{< /q >}}



We equate that to these to the 4 properties of waves and the 4 classes:

1. Worker-Commonality as Liberal (shudra caste)
2. Warrior-Density as Necocon (ksattriya caste)
3. Thinker-Balance as Conservative (brahmin caste)
4. Trader-Spread as Neoliberal (vaesha caste) 


We thus define Socratic Socialism as a system of governance which has the four virtues of Socrates for the four classes.


## Marx' Socialism Versus Socratic Socialism

Marx' definitions come from effect and not from cause. That's why there is no possibility of peace and merging of left wing and right wing of Marx. 

But there is peace and unity in Socrates' four classes model, just as the Hindu shudra, ksattriya, brahmin, and vaeshya castes form one great India (Mahabharata). 

In Marx' classification, those left and right wing cannot merge. But in Socrates' classification, the four are just parts of a whole.
