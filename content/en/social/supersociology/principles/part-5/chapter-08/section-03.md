## The Symbiosis of Capitalism, Liberalism, and Neoclassical Economics 

We defined Capitalism as an economic system that uses outside ownership to amass private profits.

This requires:
- private ownership
- free markets to allow trading of ownership
- the pursuit of profit

Private ownership naturally begins with civilization. 

Free markets began after Mercantilism, which set up the system of regulated large-scale trade, had declined in favor or Liberalism and free trade and free markets. 

The pursuit of profit was formalized by the Marginal Revolution as profit maximization. This made everyone a mini-trader. 

Private ownership merged with free markets and the pursuit of profits led to Capitalism.

## 📜 From Classical to Neoclassical
Early classical economists explained wealth through labor, production, and supply. But in the 1870s, a new school of thought emerged led by thinkers like **William Stanley Jevons, Carl Menger, and Léon Walras**. This became known as **neoclassical economics**.

It shifted the focus from just production to **individual choice, utility, and market equilibrium**. The core ideas were:
- **Utility and Value**: The value of a good is determined by how much satisfaction or “utility” it gives to buyers, not just the labor used to make it.
- **Supply and Demand**: Prices naturally adjust in a free market to balance what producers offer and what consumers want, reaching an equilibrium that maximizes efficiency.
- **Rational Actors**: Individuals and businesses act rationally to maximize their own benefit, which in turn benefits society as a whole.
- **Marginal Analysis**: Decisions are made based on the cost and benefit of one additional unit, making resource use more precise.

---

## 🛠️ How It Established Modern Capitalism
Neoclassical economics gave capitalism a clear, logical structure that helped it grow and defend itself:
- **Legitimized free markets**: It proved mathematically that unregulated exchange leads to the most efficient use of resources and the highest possible prosperity.
- **Supported private enterprise**: It argued that private ownership and competition are the best drivers of innovation, growth, and fair pricing.
- **Provided policy guidance**: Governments began using these principles to shape laws, trade rules, and tax policies that favored market activity.
- **Countered rival ideas**: By showing how markets coordinate activity without central control, it offered a strong alternative to socialist and planned economic models.

---

## 📌 Conclusion
While capitalism existed before, **neoclassical economics turned it into a consistent, widely accepted system**. It remains the dominant framework taught in universities and used in policy-making today, explaining how markets work and reinforcing the idea that free exchange and individual initiative are the keys to economic progress. -->
