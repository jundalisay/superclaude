                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Proposed <strong class="text-sky-600 dark:text-sky-400">Air was the arche</strong>. Used the quantifiable processes of condensation and expansion to explain how air changed into other elements.

                        585–525 BC
                    </div>
                </div>
            </div>
            <div class="mb-10 flex justify-between items-center w-full left-timeline">
                <div class="w-2/5 bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 rounded-2xl shadow-xl p-6 border border-gray-100 dark:border-gray-700 hover:shadow-2xl transition-all duration-300">

                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">

                        535–475 BC

                </div>

                <div class="w-2/5 bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 rounded-2xl shadow-xl p-6 border border-gray-100 dark:border-gray-700 hover:shadow-2xl transition-all duration-300">
                    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 mb-2">
                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Parmenides</h3>
                        <span class="bg-gray-100 text-gray-700 text-xs font-semibold px-3 py-1 rounded-full dark:bg-gray-700 dark:text-gray-300">The One</span>
                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        The direct opponent to Heraclitus. He argued that <strong class="text-gray-900 dark:text-gray-100">Change is an illusion and reality is a single, unchanging, and uniform sphere.</strong>
                    </p>
                    <div class="mt-4 font-mono text-sm text-gray-400 dark:text-gray-500">
                        515–445 BC
                    </div>
                </div>

                    <span class="font-bold text-white text-lg">Nous</span>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">

                    </p>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Proposed the <strong class="text-teal-600 dark:text-teal-400">Four Classical Elements (Fire, Air, Water, Earth)</strong> are the building blocks, moved by the forces of Love (unification) and Strife (separation).

                        490–430 BC
                    </div>

                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Democritus</h3>
                        <span class="bg-cyan-100 text-cyan-700 text-xs font-semibold px-3 py-1 rounded-full dark:bg-cyan-900 dark:text-cyan-300">Atomist</span>
                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Student of Leucippus. Systematically developed Atomism, proposing a deterministic world ruled by the chance collisions of atoms. Often called the "Laughing Philosopher."
                    </p>
                    <div class="mt-4 font-mono text-sm text-gray-400 dark:text-gray-500">
                        460–370 BC
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="z-10 ring-4 ring-white dark:ring-gray-900 rounded-full bg-cyan-600 w-10 h-10 flex items-center justify-center">

                    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 mb-2">
                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Arychtas</h3>

                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Not much is widely known, but associated with foundational work in <strong class="text-yellow-600 dark:text-yellow-400">Dialectics</strong> and reasoning.
                    </p>
                    <div class="mt-4 font-mono text-sm text-gray-400 dark:text-gray-500">
                        435-350 BC

                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Socrates</h3>

                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Shifted philosophy from cosmology to ethics. Developed the <strong class="text-amber-600 dark:text-amber-400">Socratic Method (elenchus)</strong>, a form of cooperative argumentative dialogue to stimulate critical thinking.
                    </p>

                        470–399 BC

                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Plato</h3>

                    </div>
                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Pupil of Socrates, founded the Academy. Developed the <strong class="text-blue-600 dark:text-blue-400">Theory of Forms</strong>, arguing that non-physical, perfect Forms represent the most accurate reality.
                    </p>

                        428–348 BC
                    </div>
                </div>
            </div>

                <div class="w-2/5 bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 rounded-2xl shadow-xl p-6 border border-gray-100 dark:border-gray-700 hover:shadow-2xl transition-all duration-300">

                        Pupil of Plato, founded the Lyceum. Developed <strong class="text-green-600 dark:text-green-400">Formal Logic</strong> and pioneered systematic studies in biology, physics, ethics, and politics. Introduced concepts of teleology (purpose).

                        384–322 BC
                    </div>
                </div>

                        360–270 BC
                    </div>
                </div>

                <div class="w-2/5 bg-white dark:bg-gray-800 rounded-2xl shadow-xl p-6 border border-gray-100 dark:border-gray-700 hover:shadow-2xl transition-all duration-300">
                    <div class="flex items-center gap-2 mb-2">
                        <h3 class="font-extrabold text-2xl text-gray-900 dark:text-white">Epicurus</h3>

                    <p class="text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-300 leading-relaxed">
                        Founded Epicureanism, teaching that the <strong class="text-rose-600 dark:text-rose-400">goal of life is ataraxia (freedom from fear) and aponia (absence of pain)</strong>, achieved by seeking simple pleasures and avoiding public life.
                    </p>
                    <div class="mt-4 font-mono text-sm text-gray-400 dark:text-gray-500">
                        341–270 BC
                    </div>
                </div>
                <div class="z-10 ring-4 ring-white dark:ring-gray-900 rounded-full bg-rose-500 w-10 h-10 flex items-center justify-center">

                        Founded Stoicism, which taught that <strong class="text-sky-600 dark:text-sky-400">virtue is the only good, people should live in accordance with nature and reason, and accept what they cannot control.</strong>

--- | --- | ---
624–546 BC | Thales of Miletus | Water was the fundamental substance (arche) of the cosmos
610–546 BC |  Anaximander Pupil of Thales | Developed a cosmological vortex
585–525 BC |  Anaximenes | Air was the arche. Used condensation and expansion to explain how air changed into other elements
570–495 BC | Pythagoras | Founded a religious and philosophical school -- numbers were the ultimate reality
535–475 BC |  Heraclitus | Change is the only reality. His arche was fire
515–445 BC |  Parmenides | Founded the Eleatic school -- change is an illusion and reality is a single, unchanging, and uniform sphere
500—428 BC | Anaxagoras | Fixed essence or substance
490–430 BC | Empedocles | Four Classical Elements moved by Love and Strife
480-420 BC | Leucippus | Founder of Atomism -- universe is made of indivisible atoms moving in a void
460–370 BC | Democritus | Student of Leucippus 
470–399 BC |  Socrates | Socratic Method
435-350 BC | Arychtas | Dialectics
428–348 BC |  Plato Pupil of Socrates | Founded the Academy. Developed the Theory of Forms -- non-physical Forms represent the most accurate reality
384–322 BC | Aristotle Pupil of Plato | Founded the Lyceum. Developed Formal Logic and systematic studies in biology, physics, ethics, and politics. Introduced concepts of teleology (purpose)
341–270 BC |  Epicurus | Founded Epicureanism -- the goal of life is ataraxia (freedom from fear) and aponia (absence of pain)
334–262 BC |  Zeno of Citium |  Founded Stoicism -- virtue is the only good, people should with nature and reason, accepting what they cannot control
360–270 BC |  Pyrrho  | Founded Pyrrhonian Skepticism -- genuine knowledge is impossible and advocated for the suspension of judgment to achieve tranquility
100–170 AD | Claudius Ptolemy | Geocentric model for the next 1,300 years in the Almagest
