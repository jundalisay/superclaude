These 2 forces are called  Yin-Yang, Shiva-Shakti, and thesis-antithesis in Hegelian Dialectic.

These create a spiraling conflict that manifests as wavelength or spin.


1. Yin and Yang

In Daoism, the universe arises from the interplay of Yin (the receptive, dark, feminine, potential) and Yang (the active, light, masculine, kinetic). 

They are not enemies but complements, each containing the seed of the other. Their ceaseless flow creates the "ten thousand things"—all of manifested reality. Here, harmony (Dao) is found in their balanced dance, not the victory of one over the other.

2. Shiva and Shakti

In Hindu Tantra, this duality becomes the cosmic couple: Shiva (pure, static consciousness) and Shakti (dynamic, creative energy). 
- Shiva is the silent witness
- Shakti is the power of manifestation

Without Shakti, Shiva is inert potential. Without Shiva, Shakti is aimless force.

Their eternal union is the universe itself—consciousness energizing into form. This mirrors Yin-Yang but frames the polarity as sacred intimacy, the source of all creation and liberation.

3. Thesis and Antithesis

Hegel formulated a dialectical process where a Thesis (an established idea or order) inevitably generates its Antithesis (its direct opposite or contradiction). 

Their fierce conflict does not seek balance but resolves into a higher Synthesis, which then becomes a new Thesis, restarting the cycle. This is not a harmonious dance but a progressive, often revolutionary, engine driving spirit (Geist) and history toward greater complexity and self-realization.
