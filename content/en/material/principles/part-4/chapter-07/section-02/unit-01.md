The aetherspace, intra-aetherspace, and inter-aetherspace facilitate the flow of electrons from atoms. This is from the 1st Rule of Motion. 

This flow action is made up of 2 subactions:


### 1. Feminization 
<!-- Displacement -->

This is called Oxidation by Physics as a Loss of Electrons, causing a molecule to be feminine or have a void.

For example: neutral sodium ($\text{v11}$) loses an electron to become a sodium ion ($\text{v11}^+$).


### 2. Masculinization 
<!-- Relocation  -->

This is called Reduction by Physics as a gain of electrons, causing a molecule to be masculine or have a plenum.

<!-- In cartesian physics the gain of electrons is from a void being filled. The loss of electrons is from space pushing out the electron. give a name for this void-filling and space-ejecting.  -->


For example: a neutral chlorine atom ($\text{v17}$) gains an electron to become a chloride ion ($\text{v17}^-$).




## A Classic Example: Making Table Salt

When sodium metal reacts with chlorine gas, they form sodium chloride ($\text{v11v17}$).

$$\text{2v11} + \text{v17}_2 \rightarrow \text{2v11v17}$$


---

We can break this down into two **half-reactions** to see the electron transfer:

- Sodium loses electrons and becomes feminine $\text{2v11} \rightarrow \text{2v11}^+ + \text{2e}^-$
- Chlorine gains those electrons and becomes masculine $\text{v17}_2 + \text{2e}^- \rightarrow \text{2v17}^-$
