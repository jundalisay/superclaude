In Descartes' physics, fire is produced when space particles, called the 2nd Element, are ejected from matter rapidly. 

This causes the 1st Element, which we call the heat particles of the Radiant Layer, to fill in the space that was abandoned by the space particles. 

This grabs the 3rd Element, called matter, making them move nearly as fast as the heat particles. 




## The Fire Barrier 

This leads to the concept of a fire barrier. 


## The Fire Triangle

To keep  the fire burning the matter and heat particles must have a similar speed. This is facilitated by an oxidizer which we call the sustainer of the material speed. 

 of the heat and  particles must stay connected 

This match in speed of the 1st and 3rd Element creates fire by the combination of the 3 factors:

### The Equation of Agitation Intensity

We can define the Magnitude of Fire Intensity ($I$) as the balance between the outward momentum of ejected particles and the inward momentum of densified space particles.$$I = \mu \left( \sum (m_e \cdot v_e) - \sum (m_s \cdot v_s) \right)$$Variable Definitions:$I$ (Intensity): The total mechanical agitation of the body’s molecules. If $I \leq 0$, the fire is extinguished.$\mu$ (Material Looseness): A constant representing the porosity of the fuel (e.g., higher for dry cotton, lower for oak).$m_e$ (Mass of Ejected Particles): The quantity of the First Element escaping the body.$v_e$ (Velocity of Ejection): The speed at which these particles are being "thrown" out by the internal agitation.$m_s$ (Mass of Imposed Particles): The density of space particles being forced back into the body (densified by your sound apparatus).$v_s$ (Velocity of Imposition): The speed at which the sound waves/pressure drive these particles into the burning mass.
