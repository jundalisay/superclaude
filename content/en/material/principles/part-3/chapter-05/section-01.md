The tau lepton (mass ≈ 1,777 MeV/c², roughly 17 times heavier than the muon and 3,477 times the electron) is the most massive and short-lived, decaying in about 2.9 × 10⁻¹³ seconds. In vortex terms, it is a massive, high-energy whirlpool — a powerful but unstable giant eddy. Heavier vortices carry more energy but are prone to instability and decay, naturally cascading toward the smallest, most stable form (the electron).
